The Programming Language RTC   Program Design

Language Elements

    

RTC consists of tokens that represent identifiers, keywords, numbers and special characters. They consists of the following characters:

·Lower and upper case characters a–z and A–Z
·Numbers 0–9
·Special characters + – * / = , . ; ? " # % & _ ' < > ( ) [ ] { } | ~ :

Other characters such as umlauts and backslash '\' are not permitted.

 

RTC distinguishes between the following language elements:

·Keywords
·Identifiers
·Numbers
·Characters and strings
·Symbols

As identifiers, the names of variables and functions are understood. They may consist of upper and lower case characters, numbers, and the underscore character '_'. The first character must be a character or the underscore.

Numbers may include a (negative) sign and – according to the data type – a decimal point. Hexadecimal numbers are preceded by the combination 0x (e.g. 0xFF).

Individual characters are enclosed in single quotes; strings in double quotes, e.g. 'A' and "Hello."

As delimiter between the individual tokens, one or more spaces or tabs or the end of a line may be used.
